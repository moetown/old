Design processes

of which I have experience in are both analouge and digital as I have a product development background with a digital design specialisation. I always prefer doing a thorough user study first and a contextual inquiry in order to really pinpoint the needs of the specific user group. This is followed by an iterative process where the user needs are incorporated into the design through an creative process with simple yet efficient means. Usually this means personas and storytelling for the description and rapid prototyping for the visualisation. The simple prototypes could then be evaluated with the users, tested for quick feedback and iterated before digitalised.

Digital mock-ups could be done in Sketch to save time and do simple beta-testing. With the new Craft plugin from Invision you can now make objects clickable and use them like you'd do in a real app or browser. When the mock-ups have been tested, they can be digitalised further with programming languages. I really believe it is important to include the customer and user throughout this process for several reasons: If something is not heading in the direction desired for either the user or the client, it needs to be discovered qickly and evaluated. If the client is 100% with you on the mindset you have set out to design with throuhgout the process, he or she will be less likely to be surprised by the outcome. Transparency, communication and openness is extremely important for satisfactory results. One of my learnings from working at Volvo is that this mindset will also save time - which in the end is money.
